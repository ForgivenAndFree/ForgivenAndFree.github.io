Brothers and sisters in Christ, I am writing this in the middle of the night because my wife is asleep and I do not want her to see the screen. I know some of you will think I have lost my senses. Please hear me out.

Last Sunday after evening service, a young man from our congregation — I will not use his name — handed me a burned CD-R with a black marker scrawl on it. One word. WASHERMAN. He said his cousin had passed it on to him, and his cousin had gotten it from a bible college roommate, and the roommate was no longer at the college.

The young man looked gray. That is the only word I have. Gray in the face, gray under the eyes, like a man who had not slept in a week. He told me, "Brother, I need you to listen to it. I cannot stop hearing it. I need somebody to tell me if I am one of them."

I asked him, "One of whom?"

He just shook his head and walked out to his truck.

⚠ A word of caution: I am going to share what happened when I listened. I am NOT going to link the audio file or give the title. If any of you know the sermon I am talking about, please do not name it in this thread. I have my reasons and by the end of this post you will understand.

Here is a picture of the CD-R he gave me. The handwriting is not his. He told me the handwriting was already on it when he got it.

I put it in the computer in my study around 9 pm. The audio was a sermon preached to a youth conference. I will not tell you where or when. You should not look for it. I am going to call the preacher the Washerman because I cannot bring myself to type his true name in this post, and because that was the word written on the CD, and because I have come to believe in the last four days that the word on the CD was not written there as a nickname but as a warning. Some of you already know exactly who I mean. Those of you who do know — you are nodding right now, and you are afraid, and you are right to be.

His voice. I do not know how to describe his voice. It is not loud at first. It is the voice of a man who has already made up his mind about you, and the thing he has made up his mind about is not good news. For the first twenty minutes I listened like I would listen to any old time preaching. I even said "amen" once.

Then he said the part about the ten. You know the part I mean if you have heard it. If you have not heard it, I am begging you as a brother: do not go looking.

After that part I could not move out of my chair. I sat in the dark in my study until 4 in the morning with the CD finished and the room silent. I kept turning it over in my head. Am I one of the nine? How would I know? What if every good thing I have ever felt about the Lord was just the stony ground? What if the fruit I thought I had was the fruit of a man pretending? What if my whole life — forty-one years of walking with Jesus — has been me fooling myself?

I want to tell you I prayed and God met me and I had peace. I want to tell you that so badly. But this is a forum of brethren and I will not lie here. I have not slept more than an hour a night since Sunday. My wife thinks I am sick. I am sick. I am sick of my own heart, because every time I look at it, the Washerman is in there looking back.

Before I close this first post I want to share something that may be nothing or may be everything. My young brother from church — the one who gave me the CD — did not come to Wednesday service. I drove out to his house Wednesday night. His truck was in the driveway. The lights were on. Nobody answered the door. I could see through the window that his Bible was open on the kitchen table, and there was a yellow legal pad next to it, and on the legal pad he had written the same two words over and over, down the whole page.

The two words were: AM I. Nothing else. Just AM I, AM I, AM I, AM I, all the way down.

His mother called me yesterday morning. She said he did not come home Wednesday. His truck is still there. His wallet is still there. His phone is still there. He is not.

I will post again tomorrow if I can. Please pray for me. Please pray for him. And please — if a young man at your church hands you a burned CD with two letters on it — do not put it in the tray.

— bro_hezekiah

Bro. Hezekiah, my hands are shaking reading your post. My hands are literally shaking and I had to go get a glass of water before I could type this.

My nephew Caleb came down from bible college at Christmas. He was on fire for the Lord when he left in August. When he came home at Christmas he was not the same boy. He would not eat at the table with us. He would sit in the guest room with his Bible on his knees and just read Romans 9 over and over and over. When I asked him what was wrong he said, "Aunt Martha, the Washerman says most of us are not real. The Washerman says the shepherds are telling the goats they are sheep. I am trying to figure out which one I am and I cannot figure it out."

I asked him who the Washerman was. He looked at me like I had slapped him. He said, "Do not ask after him in this house. Do not speak of him at all if you can help it. He hears it when you do."

I thought he was being dramatic. College boys get into these phases. I made him eat some ham and I prayed with him and I thought that was the end of it.

He went back to school on January 7th. On January 19th the dean called my sister and said Caleb had not been in class for eleven days and his dorm room was empty except for his Bible, which was open on the desk. I am going to tell you what was underlined in his Bible because I think you need to know. It was in Matthew 7. The verses from 21 to 23. He had underlined them so hard with a ballpoint pen that the page was torn through. You know the ones. Many will say to me in that day, Lord, Lord...

I never told anybody this because I was ashamed. I thought it was a mental health thing. I thought my nephew had a breakdown from studying too hard. But reading your post I am wondering if I was wrong. I am wondering if it is the same thing.

One more thing and then I will go. When Caleb was home at Christmas I went into the guest room one morning to bring him coffee. He was asleep on top of the covers with his Bible on his chest. On the inside of his forearm, in blue pen, he had written one word in block letters going from his wrist up toward his elbow. WASHERMAN. And underneath it, smaller, in the same pen: am I clean.

I will be praying for you tonight. I will be praying for your young brother. I will be praying for my Caleb who we have not heard from in seven months now.

It said, "Pastor, how do I know if I am the tenth." I did not understand the question. I replied asking him to call me. He never called. Two days later his parents came to my office and said he had locked himself in his bedroom and would not come out. He had slid a note under the door. The note said, and I quote because I kept it in my desk drawer:

"Mom dad I listened to the washerman and I know now. I was never one of his. The feeling I had at camp in 2003 was not real it was the flesh. I prayed the prayer but the prayer did not pray me. I am not going to come out until I am sure and I am never going to be sure. Please do not come in. Please do not try to talk me out of it. He is right and he knows me better than you do."

We got him out. He is at a facility now. He does not speak. He sits and mouths the same four words over and over without sound. I have watched him do it. The four words are: am I the tenth.

Here is why I am posting publicly. When I went to his bedroom after we got him out, I found his laptop open on the bed. The browser was open to a sermon audio website. The audio player was at 44 minutes and 12 seconds. Paused. I tried to close the browser and the computer froze. I pulled the battery. When I plugged it back in and turned it on, the browser opened by itself and went back to the same page. Same audio. Same 44:12. I unplugged the wireless. Same thing. I factory reset the machine the next morning at a shop. When the tech turned it on to show me it was clean, the browser opened to the same page. The tech went pale and told me to take the laptop and not come back.

I took the laptop to the church parking lot and I smashed it with a claw hammer. I buried the pieces in three different places. I am a grown man and a pastor and I am telling you on a public forum that I buried the pieces of a laptop in three different places.

The boy still mouths the words. He has not stopped in four months.

I could not sleep again. I went out to my study to read this thread and I heard something from the computer. It was not the speakers. The speakers are not even plugged in, I unplugged them Sunday night and threw the cord in the garage. It was coming from inside the tower. A man's voice. Low. Measured. Kind in that terrible way kind things can be when they are telling you something you cannot survive.

I could only catch a few words at a time. He said, "examine." He said, "are you sure." He said, "ten of you." He said my name. He said my wife's name. He has never met my wife. He does not know I exist. But the voice in the tower said her name and then it said, "she is not one of them either, brother, and you have known it for years."

I unplugged the tower from the wall. The voice kept going for eleven more seconds. I counted.

I am going to post this and I am going to walk out to the field behind my house. I am not going to do what TULIP_1689 said he was going to do. I am going to do the opposite. I am going to stand in the field and say out loud, as loud as I can, Christ died for me, a sinner, and my assurance is His blood and not my feelings. I am going to say it until the sun comes up. I am going to say it until I believe the man saying it.

If I do not post again, will one of you brothers drive out here and check on my wife. She does not know about any of this. Please do not tell her about the CD. Tell her I loved her. Tell her the Gospel with her name in it. Her name is Ruth.

Tell Ruth that Christ died for Ruth.

Bro. Hezekiah has not posted since 3:46 this morning. I called a pastor friend of mine in his county an hour ago and asked him to drive out. He called me back fifteen minutes ago.

The house is fine. Ruth is fine. She was in the kitchen making coffee when he pulled up. She said her husband had been "a little under the weather" but he was out in the field praying and she had heard him out there all morning saying the same sentence over and over. She said it with a small smile on her face because she thought it was sweet that he was praying so hard. She told my friend the sentence. It was the sentence from his last post.

My friend walked out to the edge of the field to get him for breakfast. The field is about two acres. Corn on three sides. My friend said he could hear Bro. Hezekiah's voice when he was still on the porch. He could hear it when he crossed the yard. He could hear it when he stepped into the corn. Same sentence, over and over, in Hezekiah's voice. Clear as day.

He walked the whole field. He did not find him. He walked it twice. He did not find him. The voice kept going the whole time, from every direction and from no direction, saying Christ died for me a sinner and my assurance is His blood and not my feelings, over and over, in the voice of a man who was not there.

He finally came back to the porch. Ruth was still in the kitchen. He did not know what to tell her. He said he would come back in an hour. When he came back, Ruth was sitting at the kitchen table and she said, very calmly, "He came in through the back door and he ate his eggs and he went to lie down." My friend went into the bedroom. The bed was made. Nobody had lain on it. The pillow was not dented. There was no sign that any man had been in that room that morning.

Ruth is at her sister's now. The sheriff has been out. There is no body. There is no sign. The field is just a field.

I am closing this thread. I am leaving it up as a warning. I am pinning it to the top of the subforum. New members: do not post in this thread asking for the name or the audio. Do not private message me or any member asking for the name or the audio. If you do, I will ban you without warning. I am not trying to be harsh. I am trying to love you.

For those of you who have already listened — who are reading this right now with that cold feeling in your chest — hear me one more time. The question is not am I the tenth. The question was never am I the tenth. That question has no floor. You will fall through it forever. You will count yourself onto one side and then off of it and then onto it again until you go mad, and the counting itself is the trap. The question is did Christ die for sinners, and the answer is yes, and you are a sinner, so the arithmetic goes where it goes.

Do not stand in front of the mirror. Walk through the door behind it. There is a door behind it. His name is Jesus and He is not a feeling and He is not a prayer you prayed at eleven at summer camp and He is not a sermon in your ears. He is a Man who died on a cross on a Friday and got up on a Sunday and He is coming back and when He comes back He is going to come back for sinners like you.

Now I am going to say something and I want the moderators of this forum to know that I am saying it with my eyes open and I will accept whatever discipline comes from it. I am a Baptist. I have been a Baptist pastor for twenty-six years. I have preached against infant baptism from that pulpit more times than I can count and I have told my people the Lord's Supper is a symbol and a memorial and nothing more. I am not writing to retract any of that today. That is a conversation for another week and another thread and I am not the man to have it.

But I am going to tell you the truth about what happened to me at my kitchen table on Tuesday morning. When my wife said those words to me — Christ died for sinners, you are a sinner, the math is not complicated — something in me wanted to argue back. Something in me wanted to say, but how do I know the feeling I had at the altar in 1981 was the real one. The Washerman had gotten into the place in my head where the Gospel used to live and he had set up a little courtroom in there and he was holding court. And the only thing that got me up from that table was my wife saying it out loud, in her own voice, with my name in it. Not a sermon. Not a verse I looked up. Her voice. My name. Out loud.

And I sat there afterward and I thought about that, and I thought: that is not something I have ever given my own people. I preach at them from a pulpit. I tell them to examine themselves. I tell them to make their calling and election sure. I have never once in twenty-six years looked a single member of my congregation in the eye and said, by the authority of Christ I tell you your sins are forgiven, yours, by name, today. I did not think I was allowed to. I thought that was a Romish thing. I thought the Word preached generally was enough.

Brothers, in an ordinary week I would still say the Word preached is enough. I am not writing in an ordinary week. I am writing in the week that Bro. Hezekiah walked into a field and did not walk out. And I am telling you that if you have listened to the Washerman and you are reading this at three in the morning with the cold feeling in your chest, the Word preached generally at a congregation on a Sunday morning in six days is not going to reach you in time. Something got into your ear one at a time and something has to come in after it one at a time.

So I am going to tell you what I am going to do on Sunday, and I am going to tell you what I would tell you to do if you were sitting in my office right now. On Sunday I am going to stand at the back of my sanctuary after the service and I am going to take every person who comes through that door by the hand, one at a time, and I am going to say their name and I am going to say, Christ died for you. I have never done that before. I am going to do it on Sunday. If my deacons have a problem with it they can have a problem with it.

And if you cannot wait until Sunday — and some of you cannot, and I know some of you cannot — then I am going to tell you something that is going to cost me with some of the brethren on this board and I do not care. Find a confessional Lutheran pastor. I do not mean the liberal ones. I mean the old kind, the ones who still believe every word of their book. I have one in my town. I have known him for eleven years. I have disagreed with him about the sacraments for eleven years and I will probably disagree with him about them for eleven more. But I drove to his parsonage on Tuesday afternoon after I got up from that kitchen table and I sat in his study and I told him what was happening to me and he did not argue with me and he did not examine me and he did not ask me if I was sure. He opened a little book and he read me some words and the words had my name in them and they said I forgive you all your sins in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Ghost, and I am a grown Baptist pastor and I wept in that man's study like a child, because the Washerman had no answer to a sentence like that. The courtroom in my head folded up and went quiet. It has been quiet ever since.

I am not telling you to become a Lutheran. I am telling you that in my own tradition I did not have a tool sharp enough for what got into me this week, and a brother in another tradition did, and he used it on me, and I am alive this morning because of it. Take that for whatever it is worth to you. I am going to take whatever comes from saying it on this board.

He is not in the tower of your computer at 3 am. He is not in a burned CD-R with two letters on the front. He is where He promised to be — in the Word preached, and in the water, and in the bread and the cup, and in the voice of a called man saying your sins are forgiven you out loud, with your name in it. Go find that voice. Tonight if you have to. Do not wait.
